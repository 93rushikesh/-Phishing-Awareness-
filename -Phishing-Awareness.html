What Exactly is Phishing?

Phishing is a fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, and credit card details by disguising as a trustworthy entity in an electronic communication. Attackers use deceptive emails, websites, and text messages to trick recipients into revealing their personal and financial data. It's one of the oldest and most effective forms of cyber attack.

The Psychology: Why Do We Fall for Phishing?

Phishing attacks are successful because they exploit human psychology. Attackers manipulate our natural emotional responses:

  • Fear: "Your account has been compromised!" makes us act without thinking.
  • Greed: "You have won a $1,000,000 lottery!" tempts us to click.
  • Curiosity: "See who viewed your profile" piques our interest.
  • Urgency: "This offer expires in one hour!" pressures us into quick decisions.
  • Helpfulness: "Help us verify your account details" makes us feel like we are assisting.

What to Do If You've Been Phished?

If you suspect you've fallen victim to a phishing scam, act immediately:

Disconnect: Immediately disconnect your device from the internet to prevent further malware spread.
Change Passwords: Change the password for the compromised account and any other accounts that use the same password.
Contact Your Bank: If financial information was shared, contact your bank or credit card company to report the fraud and block transactions.
Scan Your Device: Run a full scan with a reputable antivirus program to check for malware.
Report It: Report the phishing attempt to the company that was impersonated and to relevant authorities.
